January 2015

English and German

English Words of the Month


  1. Obdurate: stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion or course of action
    • Latin ob: against + durare: to harden
    • Synonyms: obstinate, callous

  2. Profligate: recklessly extravagant or wasteful in the use of resources
    • Latin pro: forward + fligare: to strike
    • Synonyms: prodigal, improvident

  3. Snake Oil: product with questionable or unveriable quality or benefit

  4. Gamut: a complete range

  5. Salutary: promoting good health and physical well-being
    • Synonyms: salubrious

  6. Gestalt: a collection of physical, biological, psychological or symbolic elements which create a whole, unified concept or pattern which is other than the sum of its parts, due to the relationship between the parts
    • German die Gestalt: shape, form

  7. Audacious: showing a willingness to take risks
    • Latin audere: to dare
    • Synonyms: enterprising

  8. Unequivocal: leaving no doubt
    • Latin ob: against + durare: to harden
    • Synonyms: indubitable, unambiguous

  9. Compendium: a collection of concise but detailed information about a particular subject
    • Latin cum: with + pendere: to weigh

  10. Canonize: regard as being above reproach or of great significance
    • Latin canon: catalog of sacred writing
    • Synonyms: consecrate, idolatrize

  11. Curt: marked by rude or peremptory shortness
    • Latin ob: against + durare: to harden
    • Synonyms: terse, brusque

  12. Goad: incite, provoke
    • Latin ob: against + durare: to harden
    • Synonyms: incentivize

  13. Lascivious: wanton, lewd, lustful
    • Latin lascivia: lustfulness
    • Synonyms: libidinous, salacious

  14. Inchoate: being only partly in existence; imperfectly formed or formulated
    • Latin incohare: to begin
    • Synonyms: incipient, nascent, embryonic

  15. Genial: friendly, cheerful
    • Latin genius: guardian spirit
    • Synonyms: amicable, convivial, blithe

  16. Platitude: an often-quoted saying that is supposed to be meaningful but has become unoriginal or hackneyed
    • Olf French plat: flat
    • Synonyms: banality, cliché

  17. Accolade: expression of praise or admiration
    • Vulgar Latin accolare: to hug around the neck

  18. Peevish: easily irritated, particularly by unimportant things
    • Latin ob: against + durare: to harden

  19. Surreptitious: kept secret, particularly because it would be disapproved of
    • Latin ob: against + durare: to harden
    • Synonyms: clandestine

  20. Bellicose: hostile in manner or temperament
    • Latin bellum: war
    • Synonyms: antagonistic, pugnacious, quarrelsome

  21. Assiduity: diligence, industry
    • Latin ad to + sedereto sit

  22. Ardent: enthusiastic or passionate
    • Latin audere: to burn
    • Synonyms: fervent, vehement, zealous

  23. Clairvoyant: able to see beyond the range of ordinary perception
    • French clair: clear + voir: to see
    • Synonyms: prescient, oracular

  24. Impish: mischievous

  25. Sycophant: servile, self-centered flatterer
    • Synonyms: brownnoser

  26. Incorrigible: unable to be corrected, improved or reformed
    • Latin in: not + corrigere: to correct
    • Synonyms: irreparable

  27. Histrionic: overly theatrical or dramatic
    • Latin histrio: actor

  28. Harbinger: a person or thing that foreshadows, announces or signals the approach of another
    • Latin canon: catalog of sacred writing
    • Synonyms: precursor, premonition, portent, augur, auspice

  29. Palliative: (of a treatment of medicine) relieving pain or alleviating a problem without dealing with the underlying cause
    • Latin palliare: to cloak

  30. Imbroglio: an extemely confused, complicated or embarrassing situation
    • Italian imbrogliare: to tangle, confuse
    • Synonyms: quandary, impasse

  31. Recondite: little known, obscure
    • Latin re: again + condere to store, hide
    • Synonyms: abstruse, esoteric
